Problem

By 2030, the world faces a shortage of 10 million healthcare workers — not because expertise doesn't exist, but because it is not intelligently coordinated. The Virtue Foundation operates in regions where facility data is fragmented, unstructured, and incomplete, making it impossible for NGO planners to identify where critical care is available and where it is dangerously absent. Lives are lost not from a lack of expertise, but from a lack of coordination.

Solution

An agentic AI intelligence layer that ingests real healthcare facility records across Ghana, extracts structured capabilities from messy free-form text using Intelligent Document Processing, and identifies medical deserts where entire populations lack access to essential services like surgery, emergency care, or maternal health — enabling the Virtue Foundation to act with unprecedented speed and precision.

Core Features
  • IDP Extraction Engine — GPT-4o-mini parses unstructured facility text into structured capabilities, procedures, and equipment with per-field confidence scores (0–99%)
  • AI Command Center — Natural language queries powered by a LangGraph multi-agent pipeline with 6 specialized sub-agents, FAISS vector search, and full agent tracing with row-level citations
  • Medical Desert Detection — 10 capability categories mapped across 16 regions; regions missing 3+ capabilities flagged as medical deserts
  • Strategic Resource Planner — Gap matrix (16×10), AI deployment recommendations, priority scoring, and 4-step plan builder
  • Interactive Map — Leaflet map with facility markers, region polygons, and desert zone overlays
  • Voice I/O — ElevenLabs speech-to-text input and text-to-speech narration for field accessibility

Impact
The platform replaces manual spreadsheet analysis with AI-driven insights, answering critical questions instantly: Which regions have zero emergency care? Where should the next surgical unit be deployed? Which facility claims don't match their data? By identifying 10 medical deserts and 67 critical capability gaps across Ghana, it provides a clear, data-driven roadmap for intervention — ensuring that healthcare investment reaches the communities where it will save the most lives. Every data point extracted represents a patient who could receive care sooner.
